=== UK FRAUD LANDSCAPE 2026 ===

SCALE OF THE PROBLEM:
- Fraud costs UK economy £14+ billion per year
- 1 in 14 adults were fraud victims in England & Wales (CSEW 2025)
- 444,000 fraud cases recorded to National Fraud Database in 2025 — highest ever
- Over two-thirds of UK scams originate from abroad (SE Asia, W Africa, E Europe, India, China)
- Only 9% of fraud victims incurred losses over £1,000 overall — but elderly victims average £4,000-£8,314 loss per scam
- Fraud = 40-45% of all crime in England and Wales

=== SCAMS TARGETING ELDERLY (65+) ===

KEY FACTS:
- An older adult becomes a fraud victim every 56 seconds in UK
- 43% of people aged 65+ believe scammers have targeted them (Age UK)
- Over 1.8 million over-65s scammed online in past year — average loss £831
- Elderly victims lose £1 million+ per day to fraud
- 2 in 3 older people targeted by scammers in last year
- 1 in 5 experience weekly scam attempts
- 65-74 age group most affected by romance scams — 75% increase year-on-year
- Many don't report due to shame/embarrassment
- Bank closures forcing elderly online — increasing vulnerability
- Social isolation exploited by scammers to build fake relationships

TOP SCAMS TARGETING ELDERLY:
1. Phone/bank impersonation — caller claims to be bank, HMRC, police. Creates urgency. Asks to move money to "safe account" or hand cash to courier. HMRC will NEVER demand immediate payment. Banks will NEVER send a courier.
2. Romance scams — online relationship built over weeks/months then requests money. Pig-butchering variant. Women over 40 most targeted. Never video calls.
3. Pension scams — cold calls offering early pension access, high returns, overseas investments. 559 pension fraud reports in 2023, victims lost £17M average £47,000 each.
4. Doorstep scams — fake tradespeople, energy suppliers, charity collectors. 5,728 cases April 2023-March 2024, £34.5M lost, victims mostly aged 70-89.
5. Investment scams — "guaranteed returns", low-risk overseas investments. £1.2 billion lost to investment scams annually.
6. Lottery/prize scams — "you've won" but must pay upfront fee.
7. Digital phone switchover scam — claiming service will disconnect unless bank details provided.
8. Courier fraud — police or bank send courier to collect cash or bank cards.

ELDERLY RED FLAGS:
- Urgency/pressure to act immediately
- Caller knows personal details (doesn't make them genuine — data breaches)
- Asks to keep call secret from family
- Requests gift card payment, crypto, or bank transfer
- Courier collecting cash or bank cards
- Too good to be true investment returns

=== SCAMS TARGETING CHILDREN & YOUNG PEOPLE (Under 18) ===

KEY FACTS:
- 839,127 British children aged 11-16 scammed online in last 12 months
- 88% of young people aged 13-21 encountered suspected fraudulent content online in past year
- 29% of young people aged 13-21 have been a victim of fraud
- 96% of 13-18 year olds transact online, 68% make purchases independently
- 14% of those spending online believe they've been defrauded
- 22% of child scams occur on gaming platforms
- 78% of child scams occur on social media
- Sextortion against under-18s rose 72% in one year
- 97% of confirmed sextortion cases involve boys

TOP SCAMS TARGETING CHILDREN/YOUNG PEOPLE:
1. Gaming scams — fake free V-Bucks, Robux, skins generators. Fake website asks for gaming login credentials then steals account.
2. Fake job/task scam — stranger on Instagram offers £150/week for liking videos. WhatsApp group of fake members creates false proof. Then demands payment fee to unlock earnings.
3. Online romance/friendship scam — fake profile builds weeks of genuine-feeling friendship. Perfectly mirrors interests. Never video calls. Eventually asks for money.
4. Sextortion — tricks young person into sending nude image then blackmails them. Report to CEOP immediately: ceop.police.uk/Safety-Centre
5. Fake giveaways — "You've won!" requires personal details or small payment.
6. Phishing links in games/Discord — fake login pages for Roblox, Minecraft, Steam, Fortnite.
7. Ticket scams — fake tickets for concerts, sports events via social media.
8. Subscription traps — free trial requires card details then auto-charges.
9. Catfishing — fake profile to extract personal info or images.
10. Finfluencer investment scams — social media "gurus" promoting fake crypto/trading.

YOUNG PEOPLE RED FLAGS:
- Any website asking for gaming password outside official app
- Free items, currency, skins with no legitimate source
- Stranger contacts out of nowhere with job offer
- New online friend who never video calls
- Any request for payment before earning money
- "Accidentally sent you a code" — forward it to me (WhatsApp OTP scam)

=== SCAMS TARGETING MIDDLE-AGED (35-64) ===

KEY FACTS:
- 31% of 18-24s and 34% of 25-34s most likely to have been scammed
- Investment fraud heavily targets 35-55 age group with savings/pension access
- Pig-butchering targets middle-aged professionals via LinkedIn, WhatsApp wrong number
- CEO/mandate fraud targets business owners and employees
- Mortgage fraud targets home buyers

TOP SCAMS TARGETING MIDDLE-AGED:
1. Pig butchering — romantic or professional contact builds trust over weeks then pushes crypto investment. Average UK loss £18,500. Originated SE Asia.
2. Investment fraud — fake trading platforms, crypto wallets, FCA-clone firms. £649M lost in UK 2024.
3. Bank transfer fraud (APP) — tricked into authorising payment. PSR mandatory reimbursement up to £85,000 since October 2024.
4. HMRC self-assessment scam — targets January/February tax deadline. 135,500 HMRC scam reports in 10 months to December 2025.
5. CEO fraud — impersonates boss via email asking for urgent wire transfer.
6. Fake invoice fraud — intercepts supplier emails, changes bank details.
7. SIM swap — convinces network to transfer number. Then intercepts banking OTPs.
8. WhatsApp family emergency — "Hi mum/dad, lost my phone, this is my new number, need money urgently"

=== TOP UK SCAM TYPES & RED FLAGS ===

Bank impersonation: asks to move money, send courier, urgent account freeze. Call 159.
HMRC: threatens arrest, demands gift card/crypto. Forward texts to 60599. Email phishing@hmrc.gov.uk
Pig butchering: romance + crypto investment, too good to be true returns. Never met in person.
WhatsApp OTP: "sent you code by mistake", asks to forward SMS code. NEVER forward.
Delivery scam: small fee to release parcel. Royal Mail/DPD/Evri never charge release fees.
Investment fraud: guaranteed returns, FCA-unregistered firm, pressure to invest fast.
Fake job: pay fee to unlock earnings, WhatsApp group with fake members.
Romance scam: never video calls, asks for money after weeks of chat.
SIM swap: sudden loss of signal, locked out of banking. Call network immediately.
Phishing: link to fake gov.uk/bank site, asks for credentials.
Tech support: pop-up saying PC infected, asks for remote access.
Pension scam: early access, high returns, overseas investment, cold call.
Doorstep scam: fake tradespeople, leftover materials, pressure to pay cash.
Lottery/prize: must pay fee to collect winnings.
Advance fee: pay upfront to receive inheritance, prize, or loan.
Council tax scam: fake HMRC/council demanding immediate payment by phone.
QR code scam: fake QR codes on parking meters, restaurants, posters.
Cryptocurrency recovery scam: fake firm claims to recover lost crypto for fee.
Deepfake/AI voice scam: AI clones voice of family member to request urgent money.

UNIVERSAL RED FLAGS:
- Urgency/threat/countdown timer
- Requests for passwords, OTP codes, sort codes, account numbers
- Payment by gift card, crypto, bank transfer to new account
- Won't video call/always has excuse
- Too good to be true
- Asks to keep secret/don't tell family
- Contacted you out of nowhere
- Knows your details (doesn't make them legitimate — data sold in breaches)
